GPA Calculation Rules — Ilia State University

Translated from the official document (source: iliauni.edu.ge).

  1. At Ilia State University, GPA (Grade Point Average) is the sum of the products of each course's Grade Point (GP) and its credits, divided by the sum of the credits of the courses the student has taken: GPA = Σ (GP of course × credits of course) / Σ (credits of course)
  2. GP (Grade Point) is the converted score of the grade received in a course, expressed on a 4-point scale.
  3. Under the GP system, grades fall into the following categories:
    • F — 0–50 points (0 points, Fail)
    • E — 51–60 points
    • D — 61–70 points
    • C — 71–80 points
    • B — 81–90 points
    • A — 91–95 points
    • A+ — 96–100 points
  4. An E-category grade (51–60) is always fixed at 1.00 GP. Grades in the D, C, B, and A categories (61 to 95 points inclusive) increase evenly in steps of 1/12 (≈0.08) per point. An A+ grade (96 to 100 points) is fixed at 4.00 GP.
  5. GPA is awarded within all academic programs at Ilia State University, except doctoral-level studies. GPA is also not awarded for certificate programs.
  6. Ilia State University issues only the final GPA, which appears in the diploma supplement. This does not apply to the single-cycle Certified Physician program.
  7. When the final GPA is calculated, fractional values are rounded to one decimal place.
